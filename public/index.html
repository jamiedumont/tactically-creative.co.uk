The Lifeblood of a Business

Quickly, ask yourself what your website does for your business. Think on these questions...

  • Does it load in under 1 second?
    • If not then...
  • ...you could be turning off valuable customers everytime they visit your site. Bounce rates, conversion rates and even revenue are all directly linked to speed; so make sure it's fast!
  • How much time do you spend developing/maintaining your website?
    • Is that too much?
  • Your website should support your business. If it requires you to spend more time babysitting it than actually doing business...well, you can see the problem. Website development shouldn't be an arduous or time-intensive process, and maintainance should be a breeze!
  • What would be the cost of your site going offline suddenly?
    • Hint: it's a lot!
  • When a site crashes, not only do you frantically throw money at the problem to get it fixed as fast as possible, you also lose every lead that could have become a customer. A website crashing is possibly the most expensive thing that can happen to a small business.
  • How much sensitive information does your site handle? How secure is it?
    • We hope "very"...
  • Everyone's data is digital these days, which means that internet crime has bigger payoffs than ever before. A site's security is always being tested by those with malicious intent, and unfortunately the web at large has a lot of catching up to do!

A business can live and die by the website they present the world. That why I presume you spend whatever time, money and energy you can spare maintaining yours? The problem is that often this distracts you from you know, actually running the business!

Your website should be your business' best asset, not your biggest source of worry.

Simply Deliver Peace of Mind

Delivering a website that accomplishes all three is incredibly complex. That complexity drives up both the cost of the system but also it's intricacy, making it very inflexible and brittle. Updating or augmenting these sites can be a minefield, and once again expensive.

What if, instead of 'adding' components and systems to make a site fast or reliable, you strip stuff away? It's been said that "simplicity is the ultimate sophistication". This applies to websites perfectly. By using simple tools, in an intelligent manner, you can build the same high-performance result; without the unnecessary cost or worry.

What if you were able to provide your business with a cost-effective site, that met these criteria and delivered complete peace of mind? How much more could you accomplish by concentrating on your business, and it's evolution?
